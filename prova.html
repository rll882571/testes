What happen to your body when you have a cold?

When you have a cold, your body is fighting a viral infection, usually caused by a rhinovirus. This is a very common illness, and the symptoms can be a real nuisance. You might feel a sore throat, have a runny or stuffy nose, and sneeze a lot. A cough and a feeling of general tiredness are also very common. Your immune system is working hard to fight off the virus. While there's no cure, you can relieve the symptoms with rest, plenty of fluids, and over-the-counter medicines. A cold usually lasts for about 7 to 10 days, and then you start to feel better as your body wins the battle against the virus.
